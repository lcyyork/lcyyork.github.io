Accurate wave function methods are often limited by their steep computational cost, which grows rapidly with system size.
A central goal of our research is to develop reduced-scaling approaches that retain the accuracy of high-level electronic-structure methods while significantly lowering their computational demands.

In the multireference (MR) context, we have developed frozen natural orbital techniques for the driven similarity renormalization group (DSRG) {% cite Li2024c %}.
By truncating the virtual orbital space based on natural occupation numbers, this approach greatly reduces the cost of dynamical correlation treatments while preserving high accuracy.
More broadly, we are interested in exploring reduced-scaling strategies to extend the reach of accurate electronic structure theory to complex chemical environments.
Examples include local correlation methods based on pair natural orbitals and tensor decomposition techniques {% cite Hannon:2016bh %}.
