Understanding reaction mechanisms at the electronic-structure level is fundamental to controlling chemical reactivity and designing new catalysts.
Mechanistic insight bridges the gap between empirical observation and molecular-level explanation by revealing key intermediates, transition states, and energy profiles that govern how and why reactions proceed.

In collaboration with Professor Changgui Zhao and Professor Guohua Hou at Beijing Normal University,
we has employed density functional theory to elucidate mechanisms of diverse organic and catalytic reactionsfor a variety of challenging organic and organometallic processes {% cite Li2024a Wang2024 Su2025 %}.
These calculations clarify competing pathways and reveal the origins of selectivity.
In the future, we plan to investigate reactions involving transition-metal complexes and photochemical processes, where strong correlation plays a central role.
