Magnetism in transition-metal complexes arises from the interplay of strong electron correlation and relativistic effects.
Accurate prediction of magnetic exchange couplings in polynuclear systems is essential for understanding spin states, magnetic communication pathways, and the design of molecular magnetic materials.

Recently, we have investigated magnetic exchange interactions in 3d bimetallic complexes {% cite Hu2026 %}.
We demonstrated that our approach yields reliable exchange coupling constants together with clear mechanistic insight into the underlying superexchange pathways.
Looking ahead, we aim to extend our methodology to the computation of magnetic response properties,
including the electronic g tensor, which plays a central role in electron paramagnetic resonance spectroscopy and the characterization of anisotropic magnetic behavior.
