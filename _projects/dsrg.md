The driven similarity renormalization group (DSRG) is a modern many-body framework for systematically incorporating dynamical electron correlation in molecular systems {% cite Li:2019fu  %}.
In DSRG, a continuous unitary transformation is used to decouple reference and excited configurations, with a flow parameter that controls the extent of correlation included.
This parameter effectively suppresses small energy denominators, leading to numerically stable and well-behaved equations.

The DSRG approach provides a flexible foundation for developing multireference methods, offering balanced treatments of dynamical and static correlation.
Its robust formulation makes it particularly attractive for strongly correlated systems, such as excited states {% cite Wang2023 %} and transition-metal chemistry {% cite Li2025 %}.
When combined with the density matrix renormalization group,
DSRG offers a scalable route to accurate multireference calculations for large active spaces containing more than 40 active orbitals {% cite Li2025 %}.
