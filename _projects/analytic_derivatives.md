Analytic energy derivatives play a central role in modern electronic structure theory, enabling efficient geometry optimizations, vibrational frequency analyses, and molecular property calculations.
Compared to numerical differentiation, analytic gradients are both more accurate and computationally efficient, avoiding the huge cost associated with finite-difference approaches.

We have derived analytic nuclear energy gradients for the second-order perturbation theory based on the driven similarity renormalization group (DSRG) {% cite Wang:2019kf Wang2021 %}.
This development makes it possible to perform reliable geometry optimizations and explore potential energy surfaces within the DSRG framework, extending its applicability beyond single-point energy calculations.

In collaboration with Professor Wenjie Dou at Westlake University, we have developed analytic nuclear gradients for the stochastic resolution-of-identity CC2 method {% cite Zhao2024d Zhao2025a %}.
By combining stochastic tensor factorization with efficient gradient theory,
these works enable cost-effective structure optimizations and excited-state studies for large molecular systems while maintaining high accuracy.
